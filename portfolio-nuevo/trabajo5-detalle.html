Gulliver’s Travels

This editorial project was created to celebrate the 300th anniversary of Gulliver’s Travels, one of the most influential works of classic literature. The goal was to design a commemorative edition that honors Jonathan Swift’s timeless narrative while offering a fresh, modern visual interpretation. The editorial concept focuses on blending historical references with contemporary graphic elements to evoke both the book’s rich heritage and its enduring relevance. Through the careful selection of typefaces, illustrations, and textures, the design conveys the adventurous spirit and satirical tone of the story. The project also incorporates thematic motifs inspired by the fantastical islands and characters Gulliver encounters, creating a cohesive visual language that enhances the reader’s immersion into the world of the novel.

The layout phase emphasized clarity, hierarchy, and rhythm to ensure a seamless reading experience while reflecting the complexity and whimsy of Swift’s text. Strict grid systems were employed to maintain consistency across chapters, allowing diverse content — from maps and illustrations to footnotes and quotes — to coexist harmoniously on each page. Typography choices balanced readability with stylistic personality, using serif fonts to evoke the classic feel of 18th-century literature, complemented by more modern sans-serifs for headings and captions. The arrangement of visual elements was carefully considered to guide the reader’s eye naturally, creating an engaging flow that respects the narrative’s pacing. Overall, the layout design sought to merge tradition and innovation, making this anniversary edition both a tribute and a fresh interpretation for contemporary audiences.
